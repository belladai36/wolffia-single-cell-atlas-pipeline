# Candidate Cell Programs for Wolffia Prediction

## Purpose

This document defines the first-pass set of candidate cell programs we want to track in `Wolffia australiana` using public plant single-cell references.

These categories are intentionally broad. They are meant to guide marker collection and hypothesis generation before any Wolffia single-cell data are available.

## Program Categories

### 1. Proliferative / Meristematic Programs

Core question:

- does Wolffia retain a clearly identifiable proliferative population analogous to meristematic or stem-like plant cells?

Why it matters:

- this is one of the strongest tests of whether developmental patterning is conserved in a highly reduced flowering plant

Expected evidence:

- cell cycle genes
- DNA replication genes
- chromatin and division-associated genes

### 2. Epidermal / Surface Identity Programs

Core question:

- are there surface-associated transcriptional programs that remain distinct despite strong body-plan reduction?

Why it matters:

- if present, they may help define boundary, protective, or exchange-related functions in a very small aquatic plant body

Expected evidence:

- cuticle-associated genes
- outer-cell-layer markers
- transport and stress-interface genes

### 3. Photosynthetic / Assimilation Programs

Core question:

- does Wolffia maintain one or more transcriptionally distinct photosynthetic states?

Why it matters:

- this is likely to be one of the easiest conserved programs to detect
- it may also reveal how photosynthetic function is organized in a minimal plant body

Expected evidence:

- chloroplast-associated genes
- light-harvesting genes
- carbon fixation genes

### 4. Vascular-Like / Transport Programs

Core question:

- are transport-associated programs present as distinct states, or are they reduced or merged with other cell identities?

Why it matters:

- Wolffia lacks obvious large-scale organ complexity, so vascular-like signatures are especially interesting as a test of hidden cellular complexity

Expected evidence:

- solute transport genes
- cell-wall specialization genes
- known xylem/phloem-associated markers from reference plants

### 5. Developmental Transition Programs

Core question:

- does Wolffia show discrete cell types, continuous trajectories, or a mixture of both?

Why it matters:

- developmental simplification may appear as broad transitional states rather than many sharply separated clusters

Expected evidence:

- gradient-like expression programs
- mixed lineage markers
- pseudotime-compatible transcriptional shifts

### 6. Reproductive / Floral Programs

Core question:

- are reproductive precursor or floral-associated transcriptional programs detectable, even if flowering is rare or condition-specific?

Why it matters:

- reproductive signatures could reveal whether rare developmental modes remain transcriptionally accessible in Wolffia

Expected evidence:

- floral regulator orthologs
- reproductive development markers
- stress- or induction-associated flowering programs

### 7. Aquatic Adaptation / Stress-Responsive Programs

Core question:

- does Wolffia contain plant states that are better explained by aquatic adaptation than by canonical terrestrial plant cell types?

Why it matters:

- these may become the most biologically novel part of the project

Expected evidence:

- nutrient uptake programs
- redox and stress-response genes
- aquatic or floating-plant-associated transport signatures

## Priority Ranking for Early Analysis

These are the categories most likely to be informative in the first pass:

1. proliferative / meristematic
2. photosynthetic / assimilation
3. vascular-like / transport
4. developmental transition programs

These are important but may be harder to interpret early:

1. epidermal / surface identity
2. reproductive / floral
3. aquatic adaptation / stress-responsive

## How This Will Be Used

For each category, we will eventually collect:

- reference marker genes
- Wolffia ortholog candidates
- expected expression pattern
- confidence level
- validation strategy

The resulting marker table will support the first hypothesis-driven interpretation of future Wolffia single-cell expression data.
